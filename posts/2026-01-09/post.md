Here are some ways folks local to the metro can get involved, *in order of increasing exposure and danger to yourself:*

0. Understand where ICE is operating via sites like [IceOut](https://iceout.org/en/).
1. Attend a constitutional observer training hosted by [Monarcha](https://monarcamn.org/) or [MIRAC](https://www.miracmn.com/).
2. Attend passive protests. These will be broadcast via the usual progressive channels such as [Indivisible](https://indivisible.org/), [AFL-CIO](https://mnaflcio.org/), [MIRAC](https://www.miracmn.com/), [TCDSA](https://twincitiesdsa.org/), and [COPAL](https://copalmn.org/). A great way to discover more local groups is to simply ask around at these protests. 
3. Report confirmed ICE/CBP/FBI vehicle sightings to the above groups and online databases, such as the [MN ICE Sighting License Plate Submission](https://airtable.com/apprPhYEXiE9svCFc/pagzgcHmtZuMAMvZ1/form). You can crosscheck plates with the [StopICE plate tracker](https://www.stopice.net/platetracker/?about=1).
4. Participate in local ICE watch groups. You will need to discover these by talking to other protestors and your neighbors as they are mostly on private, E2E encrypted applications like Signal. There are roles for those who are stationary, those who are on foot, those on bike, those with vehicles, and those who can act as dispatchers from home.
5. Harass fascists as they attempt kidnappings. You can impede their work in a legal manner by recording their actions, honking car horns, blowing whistles, and getting the word out as fast as possible to your local groups. Note that these are thugs, not officers. Many of them were simply handed a gun and a vest in the last few months. Just because you are acting within your rights does not mean those rights will be respected. Danger of arrest, injury, or death increases with proximity to these individuals as well as with the tension in a situation. Be aware of moving vehicles, agitators, and emotions. Do not bring pets, children, or anything that could be construed as a weapon to these interactions.
6. Protest at the [Whipple federal building](https://maps.app.goo.gl/kExCzqtNYYdQ7KZ5A). This building is easily accessed by taking the [Metro Blue Line](https://www.metrotransit.org/route/blue) from any north station south to the Fort Snelling stop. There are three entrances and exits to this facility all located along Federal Drive. I would consider this the most dangerous place in the metro right now, but also the most effective place to blockade and disrupt operations.

As with all protests, it is safer with an organized group. Be aware that the fascists will meet you a mix of physical force, gas, less-lethal and fully lethal weaponry depending on how threatened they feel. You can expect tear gas, pepper spray, shoving, and general violence at any action that obstructs their mission to terrorize Minneapolis. # Ways to keep yourself safe

- Be aware of attempts by the fascists to [kettle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kettling) you. Leave as soon as possible if there is only one exit remaining.
- Be aware that the transit systems can stop running at any time, so have a backup exit plan for any action. This is *especially* true for the Whipple building along Federal Drive, which is boxed in and in a remote area. The Blue Line is the best way in right now, but do not rely on the train alone.
- Do not bring your phone to 'hot' protests. If you do, turn off location services and put it in airplane mode due to the risk of [Stingray profiling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stingray_phone_tracker). You should also turn off biometric phone access. On iPhones, this can be be done instantly by tapping the lock button five times in a row. A full list of security measures can be found on [Proton's blog](https://proton.me/blog/how-to-protect-privacy-at-protests).
- Know the contact numbers for loved ones and legal representation, or write them on your arm in pen.
- Have a plan to take care of loved ones and pets should you find yourself detained or incapacitated. Set red lines for yourself; decide what are you willing and unwilling to confront on a given day and leave if these lines are crossed.
- If you are grabbed or they attempt to pull you out of the crowd you can often attempt escape back into the crowd until downed or handcuffed. At that point it is best to become passive, comply with orders, say nothing, and ask for a lawyer until you get one. Most protestors are being released after a 24 hour hold at this stage.
