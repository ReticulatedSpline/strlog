# ways to keep yourself safe

- Be aware of attempts by fascists to [kettle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kettling) you. Leave as soon as possible if there is only one exit remaining.
- Be aware that the transit systems can stop running at any time, so have a backup exit plan for any action. This is *especially* true for the Whipple building along Federal Drive, which is boxed in and in a remote area. The Blue Line is the best way in right now, but do not rely on the train alone.
- Do not bring your phone to hot protests. If you do, turn off location services and put it in airplane mode due to the risk of [Stingray profiling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stingray_phone_tracker). You should also turn off biometric phone access. On iPhones, this can be be done instantly by tapping the lock button five times in a row. A full list of security measures can be found on [Proton's blog](https://proton.me/blog/how-to-protect-privacy-at-protests).
- Know the contact numbers for loved ones and legal representation, or write them on your arm in pen.
- Have a plan to take care of loved ones and pets should you find yourself detained or incapacitated. Set red lines for yourself; decide what are you willing and unwilling to confront on a given day and leave if these lines are crossed.
- If you are grabbed or they attempt to pull you out of the crowd you can often attempt escape back into the crowd until downed or handcuffed. At that point it is best to become passive, comply with orders, say nothing, and ask for a lawyer until you get one. Most protestors are released after 4-24 hour detention.

# semantics
There is an understandable resistance to calling these under-trained "agents" fascists. Nobody wants to be the boy who cried wolf, and the United States is still missing some key tenets of fascism today. I see more parallels to the void ideology and public looting behavior of the [Porfiriato](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Porfiriato) than the Third Reich, for example. Even if recent actions to not yet rise to the level of fascism, I will contend that Operation Metro Surge marks a turning point in the national climate -- one in which the authorities remain defiant rather than apologetic in the face of embarassing uses of violence. In contrast to past mistakes, there was an immediate attempt by the regime to cover up the facts, deny local authorities access to evidence, and to smear the dead as terrorists even when multiple camera angles and witnesses proved beyond a shadow of a doubt what actually happened.
